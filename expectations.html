1. What is chiropractic?

Chiropractic care recognises the crucial role the spine and nervous system has in maintaining health and ensuring our bodies function optimally. At its core, chiropractic emphasises the body's innate capacity to heal itself. Rather than focusing on treating specific conditions, my primary goal is to minimise nerve interference, allowing your body to function at its best. Improving how your body functions can enhance your quality of life and can help you towards achieving your health goals. Those who want to get the most out of their body and life, choose regular chiropractic care.

2. How often will I need to attend?

I understand that everyone is different, and things like your health goals, stress levels, age, past injuries, lifestyle, and overall self-care all affect how long it takes to restore balance and improve your nervous system function. Often, pain shows up last but tends to be the first thing to go. While you might feel relief after just one adjustment, my approach is about more than just quick fixes—I’m here to help you truly thrive.

I see a lot of women through their pregnancy and postpartum, and often end up adjusting their little ones too! Others make chiropractic part of their regular wellness routine. Staying on top of your health is often easier than waiting for problems to arise. Achieving your best health takes time, and I’m here to support you every step of the way. You deserve to feel and function at your best!

3. Do I need an X-ray or MRI?

This will be evaluated on an individual basis. Should they be clinically indicated I can arrange these for you.

4. Does treatment hurt?

No, chiropractic care is not painful. I use techniques tailored specifically to you. Adjustments are powerful and effective, yet gentle. Additionally, we use different methods for babies and children compared to adults. I work on babies with the same pressure you would check the ripeness of a tomato.

5. Will I hear my bones 'crack'?

During an adjustment, you might hear a popping or cracking sound, depending on the technique used. This noise is just a release of gas from the joint and isn’t painful—in fact, many find it quite pleasant! There are several alternative techniques available that don’t produce that noise.

6. Why are adjustments so quick?

7. How will I feel afterwards?

In my experience most people feel great after an adjustment. Sometimes, engaging in light activity post-adjustment can be beneficial. For instance, a brief walk or some gentle stretching can be quite helpful. It's a good idea to avoid excessive sitting, eat healthily, stay hydrated, and ensure you get a good night's sleep. Sometimes people can feel tired or a little achy but often this is a good sign that your body has started to respond to the adjustments. Remember, while the adjustment helps get your body moving, it's up to you to maintain that movement!

8. What is the difference between chiropractic, physiotherapy and osteopathy?

Chiropractic, physiotherapy, and osteopathy all aim to enhance health and function, but they differ primarily in their underlying principles. Chiropractic focuses specifically on the function of the nervous system. By eliminating interference in the nervous system, chiropractic aims to support the optimal expression of life. Chiropractors seek to identify and address areas of dysfunction in the spine and nervous system. Adjustments are used to correct these area’s of dysfunction, with the goal of improving spinal and nervous system function.

9. How much education do chiropractors have?

All chiropractors have completed a minimum 4 year chiropractic degree at university and hold an honorary Doctor of Chiropractic title. We are fully trained in diagnosing, taking and reading x rays and developing treatment protocols. I am registered through the General Chiropractic Council (03003) with requirements to perform Continued Professional Development each year. I am fully certified in the Webster Technique and have completed my certification with the ICPA in 2018 (International Chiropractic Pediatric Association). I am also registered with the Pelvic Partnership and the United Chiropractic Association.

10. Do I need to be referred by my GP?

No, you do not need to be referred to us by your GP. Some private health insurances may require a letter to be obtained from your GP before attending for care. Please speak to your health insurance companies regarding this.

11. Where are we located/parking?

The studio is situated in a beautifully converted barn nestled between Leigh and Brockham, surrounded by the beautiful Surrey Hills. I feel very lucky to have such a great space. Free parking is available on-site. Our location is conveniently close to the surrounding villages of Leigh, Brockham, Betchworth and Newdigate and towns of Dorking, Reigate, Crawley, and Horsham, I therefore see people from Surrey, East and West Sussex and beyond.

12. Can I use my medical insurance for my chiropractic care?

Most health insurance plans will cover chiropractic care. I have chosen not to register with BUPA or AXA PPP. For all others, you pay up front and we will provide you with invoices that you use to claim back. Please speak to your health insurance company for more information.

14. What if I need to cancel?

I get it—life happens! Kids get sick, babies have those messy “poonamis” right before you leave, or antenatal appointments run late. If you can, please try to give me at least 24 hours' notice if you need to reschedule. This helps free up the spot for someone else. You can easily reschedule on the app from your sofa—or even at 2 a.m. if needed! If it’s short notice, I’ll do my best to still fit you in. Just keep in mind, cancellations or missed appointments with less than 24 hours' notice will result in a full charge.

15. How to best contact me?

The app and online booking are super efficient, but I get it—sometimes you just want to talk to a real person! Feel free to call or WhatsApp me on my business number. I’ll get back to you as soon as I can, just keep in mind I might be with patients or at home with my kids!

16. My fees

I’ve always wanted to keep fees affordable for both individuals and families, which is why I offer different pricing options starting at £50 per adjustment. You can pay through the online portal or the app (once you’re an existing patient), and we also accept cash or card. Our App uses Stripe so your card details can be saved so this makes payment even easier. Happy days!
